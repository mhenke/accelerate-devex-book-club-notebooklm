
# The "Faux Agile" Playbook

Welcome to the world of "Faux Agile," where we talk the talk of agility but walk the walk of rigid, bureaucratic processes. Hereâ€™s how you can master the art of looking agile without actually changing anything important.

## Key Principles of Faux Agile

1.  **Sprints are just mini-waterfalls.**
    *   Spend the first week in planning meetings.
    *   Spend two weeks writing code without talking to anyone.
    *   Spend the last week in a frantic rush to test and integrate everything.
    *   Call it a "sprint."

2.  **Stand-ups are for status reporting to managers.**
    *   Each person must give a detailed account of everything they did yesterday and everything they will do today.
    *   Go around the room in a circle. No interruptions.
    *   The Scrum Master is there to enforce the rules and take notes for management.

3.  **The backlog is a black hole.**
    *   The Product Owner is the only one who can add or prioritize items.
    *   The development team has no say in what they work on.
    *   Estimates are treated as commitments, and any deviation is a sign of failure.

4.  **Retrospectives are for blaming people.**
    *   Find a scapegoat for why the sprint failed.
    *   Generate action items that no one will ever follow up on.
    *   Repeat the same mistakes in the next sprint.

5.  **Agile is a process, not a mindset.**
    *   Follow the ceremonies religiously.
    *   Don't worry about the underlying principles of collaboration, customer value, or continuous improvement.
    *   If anyone questions the process, tell them they are "not being a team player."

## How to Tell if You're Doing Faux Agile

*   You have a "hardening sprint" at the end of every release.
*   Your "user stories" are just tasks in disguise.
*   You need to get approval from three different committees to change the color of a button.
*   Your team is "agile," but the rest of the organization is not.
